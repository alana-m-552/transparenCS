Guide to MIT Classes!

How to find and cross-register for MIT classes

Before Registration

  1. Check the Common CS Courses page first to find courses you might want to take and their Wellesley equivalents.
  2. Find the class on the MIT course catalog to check times and recitation options that work for you. (Make sure to take into account shuttle bus times and other logistics!)
  3. Be sure to check deadlines for both the MIT Academic Calendar and Wellesley Academic Calendar! You have to meet the earlier of the two deadlines for cross-registration, as well as for adding/dropping classes.

Registering for an MIT class

  1. When you’ve decided on what class to take, create a transfer credit request and fill out the application.
    • This involves where the course is held (what institution), course details, total contact hours (time spent in class), etc. All of this information can be found in the MIT course catalog for your course.
    • Note: You can take the course without this request, but the course will not count towards what you want it approved for, like a distribution requirement or a major requirement.
  2. Fill out the cross-registration form on the Wellesley website. Also print out the form and have your MIT instructor sign it to confirm your attendance to both schools’ registrar offices.
  3. MIT will send you a non-institute biographic form for you to fill out.
  4. Email both the cross-registration form and the non-institute biographic form to records@mit.edu; you will receive email confirmation when they receive these forms.
  5. Sign up for a MIT Kerberos account. You will receive an email inviting you to register with an activation code. Once you get an MIT account, you will need to install MIT certificates into your browser.
  6. FOR COVID: Go to http://covidapps.mit.edu/ for COVID-19 testing instructions and download the Covid Pass app. You will have to upload proof of vaccination, booster shots, and your flu shot. You will also still have to undergo COVID testing at MIT.
  7. When you have access to campus, you can get your MIT ID card at the MIT Card Services in Room E17-106.
  8. Make sure to add emergency contact information on WebSIS!

Dropping an MIT class

  1. To drop an MIT class, you have to notify both MIT and Wellesley that you are dropping a course. You can drop an MIT class through Workday on Wellesley.
    • Note: You can also credit-non an MIT class through Workday.
  2. For MIT, you can send an email records@mit.edu with the subject title, subject number, and MIT ID number.
