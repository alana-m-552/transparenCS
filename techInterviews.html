Guide to technical interviews!

What technical interviews are and how to prepare for them

What is a technical interview?

A technical interview is an interview in which your potential employer will gauge your technical skills relative to the position you are applying for in order to determine if you are the best fit for a position.

This may involve different kinds of assessments around debugging code, doing a simple coding problem, building a small project, or explaining a technical problem to the employer.

Before the interview

  • Ask the employer about what you might expect from the technical interview. Review their expectations and prepare any materials they ask you to bring.
  • Practice with building projects and doing coding challenges.
  • You can also try doing mock interviews!
  • Come up with a narrative for your coding skills and knowledge, like a context in which you were able to gain a skill you currently have.

During the interview

  • Ask for clarification on a problem if necessary.
  • Take your time to understand the problem presented to you. Donâ€™t be afraid to talk yourself through the problem!
  • Identify the core goal of the problem and come up with a step-by-step plan to reach that goal.
  • At the same time, also try to walk the interviewer through your approach to the problem. That is what they are assessing!
  • Once you are done with the basic idea of what you want to accomplish, try to optimize your answer so it is as efficient as possible.

After the interview

  • Send a thank you email to the hiring manager/your interviewer!
  • Wait to hear back from the employer about any next steps!
  • If you get rejected, consider this interview as practice for the future. There are still plenty of opportunities out there!
